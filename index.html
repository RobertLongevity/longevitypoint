A Different Way of Thinking

Better questions lead to better marketing

Most marketing conversations in senior living start in the wrong place. Here's what changes when you lead with the right question.

Old Question
"How do we get more leads?"
Better Question
"Where is performance actually breaking — and is marketing even the right lever?"
Old Assumption
"More spend means more move-ins."
Better Question
"Are we losing to competitors on budget or on rank? The answer changes everything about how you invest."
Old Pressure
"Sales needs to close better."
Honest Question
"Is this a sales problem — or a positioning or pricing gap that no closer can overcome?"
Old Decision
"Add spend across the portfolio."
Smarter Approach
"Which communities will convert more investment — and which have a ceiling no budget can lift?"
Old Metric
"Our conversion rate is too low."
Root Cause
"Conversion is a symptom. What's the actual constraint — demand, lead quality, tour experience, or the asset?"
Old Conversation
"Marketing needs more budget."
Better Conversation
"Here's where performance is constrained, here's what it would take to move it, and here's what no budget can fix."

My Approach

Figure out what's broken before deciding how to fix it

That sounds obvious. But most of the time in this industry, the prescription comes before the diagnosis. I work the other way around.

01

Listen & Diagnose

Before I recommend anything, I want to understand where performance is actually breaking — across demand, lead quality, sales execution, and asset fit. The constraint drives everything else.

02

Get Clear on the Real Problem

There's a big difference between losing business because your budget is too small and losing it because your competitive rank isn't there. I help you separate the two so your response fits the problem.

03

Build a Plan That Holds Up

The output is something you can act on and defend — in a budget conversation, a board meeting, or just getting your team aligned on what to do next.
